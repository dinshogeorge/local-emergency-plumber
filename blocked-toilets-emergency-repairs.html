Blocked Toilet Emergency Repair Services in Auckland

Blocked toilets are messy to deal with and can get worse if not properly treated. If you have a blocked toilet that needs emergency repairs anywhere in Auckland, call us right away.

What causes blocked toilets?

One of the major reasons for blocked toilets is caused by flushing too much toilet paper at once. Flushing of items like Paper towels, toys, floss, sanitary napkins, diapers, and baby wipes can lead to clogging when accumulated in the S-traps that restrict water passage. S-traps are designed to block the sewer gases from entering the room. Due to the accumulation of the flushed objects in the S-trap, a bad odour is released through vents and fixtures. Old pipework can be an issue as the pipeline may corrode or have leakage which may lead to the formation of sediments causing a blockage. Hard water can also contribute to blockage as it causes a build-up of calcium and minerals on the pipes. Some issues may not be easily visible as problems in the main sewer line which requires the supervision of an experienced plumber. We have the right team to resolve any plumbing emergency to save your money and time.

When to call an emergency plumber for a blocked toilet?

1. Overflowing toilet

A full or overflowing toilet is a sure indication of a blocked toilet which causes major health issues if not dealt with early. After the toilet is flushed, the water tends to overflow from the top of the bowl due to the obstruction in the S-trap. This blockage happens mostly with the clogging of toilet paper, diapers, sanitary napkins being flushed. This can develop into a serious menace if not consulted with an experienced emergency plumber.

2. Foul Smell

If you are dealing with a bad smell coming out of the toilet for days or weeks, you should have it checked as quickly as possible because foul smells can be an indication of blocked toilets. Due to clogging, the sewer gases from the drain are pressurized upon the vent pipes which in turn is emitted through the fixtures in the toilet. So it is recommended to seek professional assistance urgently before it's too late.

3. Slow drains

A Slowing-draining toilet is a sign that there is some kind of obstruction in the drain. As a result of this, the water stays up in the toilet bowl for a long period after you flushed. Slow draining of the water can turn into major consequences in the future. So it is better to have it fixed sooner than later.

4. Gurgling sound

Gurgling sound from the shower or tap after you flush the toilet is often due to blockage in the pipeline. Due to clogging, the air in the line is pushed backward instead of flowing through the drains causing gurgling sounds from taps or showers in your household.
