Drain Unblocking Emergency Services In Auckland

Starting with minor issues, blocked drains may turn into major problems over time which can be very frustrating. So when the sewers block, it's an emergency. With the best in-town emergency drain unblocker, we can help to unblock your drains or sewer pipes free from debris.

What causes sudden blockages in drain & sewer lines or pipes

Some blocks may unblock themselves without the inspection by homeowners or professionals. But some blockages can cause serious damage to the drainage system that requires immediate and professional help. The common factors that lead to a sudden blockage in drain and sewer lines are:

1. Tree root invasion

Trees growing near the drainage is a major threat to the sewer lines or pipes which can cause blockage to the system. The tree roots entering the underground pipes widens the already existing cracks or holes making it a favorable place to grow further. These roots obstruct the water flowing through the sewer pipes.

2. Hair

Even if the drains have guards to block hair from getting into the drainage system, some hairs will still escape through it which when combined with other greasy things can cause blockage in the sewer lines.

3. Dirt

Be it shower or sink, Drains are designed to wash off any kind of dirt. Excessive dirt washed off from your clothes or body can build up issues in the drain. The food wastes that you wash off the kitchen sink are also prone to create a blockage, especially rice, pasta, and oily foods as they can solidify in water obstructing the free flow of water through the pipes.

4. Soap

Soap can cause clogging as it is made of fat which when combined with minerals in water builds up hard residue which is capable of forming an obstruction in the pipes. These obstructions can also slow down the draining of water on showers due to the block in the water passage of the sewer lines.

5. Flushing of toilet paper and other items

Toilet papers usually don't cause clogging as it easily dissolves in water. But if it's flushed too many, all at once can surely make the drains clogged. This is not as serious as flushing other non-flushable items like sanitary napkins, diapers, floss, and toys down the drain which can get into your drainage system and wreak havoc.

6. Mineral build-up

Hard water itself is capable of causing blocked drains. Hard water has a high amount of calcium and minerals which leaves insoluble particles and gets accumulated in the pipes over time causing clogging of sewer lines.

7. Old pipe work

Older pipe works can corrode over time as their preventive coating fades. Due to corrosion, the pipe develops cracks and holes which later cause the pipeline to collapse blocking or restricting the flow of water.

Drain repair and replacement services

1. CCTV camera drain inspection

With this advanced technology of CCTV Camera drain Inspection, plumbers can easily locate the issue inside the pipelines and fix it without having to dig your yard or floor. The modern software like CUES, a self-propelled camera can be inserted inside the pipelines which help to detect the issue. Together with experienced plumbers, CCTV camera inspection is cost-effective and takes up less time to pinpoint the location of a crack and resolve it as fast as possible.

2. Drain Jetting

By using the hydro-jetting equipment, it can wash off any kind of obstructions in the line. The high-pressure water from the equipment helps in clearing out all the blockages caused due to the accumulation of dirt, hair, soap, toilet paper, and other substances stuck in the drain pipes making a clear passage for the free flow of water.

3. Tree root cutting

Tree roots growing near the drainage system is a major thing to worry about as trees tend to grow towards water sources causing a severe blockage in the sewer pipes. Cutting the roots at the earliest can help the drain pipes from collapsing and creating further complications in the future.

4. Drain relining

Drain relining is a non-dig technology widely used to repair damaged drainage pipes by inserting a new pipe into the existing one. Inserting new lining can help prevent further damage to the drainage system. It is a much more cost-effective method of drain repair when compared to the other traditional methods of fixing pipes.
