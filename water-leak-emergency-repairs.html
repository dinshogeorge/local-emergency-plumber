Emergency water leak detection & repair services in Auckland

No matter how big or small a leak may be, it should be taken into consideration. A small leak left unattended can waste thousands of liters of freshwater a day with no consideration for its future effects.

Leaks that require urgent care

1. Household leaks

Homeowners tend not to pay much attention to slow dripping droplets that are commonly found in kitchen and toilet faucets. However, these droplets combined together can result in a significant amount of water wastage. So, fixing each and every leak as soon as you spot it, can help you cut down on your water bill and save you time.

2. Underground Water Leaks

Leaks from underground pipes tend to go unnoticed since they are buried deep down the earth. If you notice any wet spots in your driveway, the underground pipe must have some form of leak which can lead to water pooling around your property. Over time, they can become a major problem, weakening the foundation of the house or building.

3. Water Main Leak

Monitoring your water bill and other signs of leakage may help you identify the leaks in the water mains. It is a large pipe that connects to a service pipe. If leakage occurs on your side of the pipe, it is your sole responsibility to have it fixed whereas if it's the problem with the service pipe, then it is to be fixed by the water council of the city. Being the main water source to your home, water main should be inspected regularly to ensure water supply without any wastage.

Leaks that require urgent care

1. Old pipes

Old pipe works develop rust over time as their protective coating begins to thin, forming pin holes through which water leaks. The tiny holes eventually evolve into bigger ones with high or low pH levels in your water. Although corrosion is an inevitable issue, insulating the pipes with metal can help to maintain their durability.

2. Leaks from joints due to high pressure

High water pressure can trigger water leaks when it’s above the maximum withstanding capacity of the pipes. Regulating the water pressure is a difficult task that can only be suggested by experienced plumbers. If not treated quickly, Small leaks can develop into major ones resulting in severe damage to the pipeline system.

3. Ground digging for building purposes

Without doing the proper examination of the pipeline system, the soil excavation for the construction of buildings or structures can sometimes lead to massive water leakage. When the ground gets disturbed, the underground pipelines will twist and bend resulting in the wastage of water. Hence it is important to do a proper survey of underground pipeline systems before doing any kind of excavation.

4. Tree root intrusion

Trees growing near the pipelines pose a severe threat to the underground pipeline system. Tree roots may invade your underground pipes causing cracks in its joints. The cracks through which the root enters also pave the way for sedimentation to form. Excessive sediment build-up can make it difficult for the flow of water to the sewer lines.

5. Faulty joints

If you notice any strange noise like ticking noise when using hot water or gas then the main culprit would be the pipe joints. As joints are the weakest spot of a pipeline system the joints will be prone to damage with high fluid pressure over time. Hence it is recommended to have your pipeline system checked by a professional plumber once a year to prevent further damage to the system.

Signs of water leaks

  • ● Water stains
  • ● Strange noises
  • ● Rusty pipes
  • ● High water bill
  • ● Musty smell
  • ● Wet spots
  • ● Cracks in foundation

Our leak detection method for underground leaks

Acoustic Leak Detection

The most modern technology to detect underground leaks is acoustic leak detection used in both residential as well as commercial plumbing. By moving this device over the pipeline, it helps to pinpoint the exact location of leakage and resolve it immediately. Our bunch of trained plumbers is well versed in acoustic leak detection to perform emergency leak detection without digging up the whole yard saving your time and money.

Underground Water leak repairs

Underground water leakage should never go unchecked as it could damage the whole house or building. Repairing the leakage whether small or big is the only remedy to have your place safe and secure. For underground water leak repairs, we use the technologically advanced form of acoustic leak detection that traces the source of leaks in no time. The mobile sensor in the device records the sound of the water flowing through the pipes and any difference in the water pressure like hissing sound helps to identify the leaking spot. After locating the leak, our specialized plumbers dig that particular area and replace the portion of pipe that leaks with a new one. Mostly older pipelines have more chance of leaking.
